# Week 1 – The Math of Uncertainty

## Overview
This week focused on building intuition for probability and uncertainty, which form the mathematical foundation of Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) and Diffusion Models. The emphasis was on understanding concepts rather than implementation.

## Topics Studied

- Gaussian (Normal) Distributions
- Bayes' Theorem
- KL Divergence
- Probability Distributions
- Latent Variable Models
- Intuition behind probabilistic machine learning

## Resources Explored

- 3Blue1Brown – Bayes' Theorem: The Geometry of Changing Beliefs
- 3Blue1Brown – Why π Appears in the Normal Distribution
- StatQuest – KL Divergence Explained
- Additional readings on latent variable models and VAEs

## Key Learnings

### Gaussian Distribution
- Understood the concept of mean and variance.
- Learned why Gaussian distributions appear frequently in nature and machine learning.

### Bayes' Theorem
- Learned how prior beliefs are updated using observed evidence.
- Developed intuition for conditional probability and inference.

### KL Divergence
- Studied KL Divergence as a measure of how one probability distribution differs from another.
- Understood its importance in Variational Autoencoders (VAEs).

### Latent Variables
- Learned how complex data can be represented in a lower-dimensional latent space.
- Built intuition for representation learning used in generative models.

## Outcome

Developed a strong conceptual understanding of probability theory, Bayesian inference, and KL divergence, providing the mathematical foundation required for deriving and implementing Variational Autoencoders in subsequent weeks.

## Deliverables

No coding assignment was required during this week. The focus was on theory, intuition, and mathematical foundations.
